## What is a Genesis Master Prompt?

### **Definition**

A **Genesis Master Prompt** is a **living document** that contains the complete context of your project, including:

1. **Project Vision**: What you're building and why
2. **Current State**: What's been done so far
3. **Decisions Made**: Key choices and their rationale
4. **Open Questions**: What still needs to be resolved
5. **Next Steps**: What to work on next

### **Analogy**

Think of it as a **"project memory"** that you share with AI at the start of every conversation.

**Without Genesis Master Prompt**:
- AI: "What can I help you with today?"
- You: "Continue working on my meditation app"
- AI: "What meditation app? Tell me about it"
- You: *Spends 30 minutes re-explaining everything*

**With Genesis Master Prompt**:
- You: *Paste Genesis Master Prompt*
- AI: "I understand your meditation app project. You're at the backend API phase. Should we continue with the user authentication endpoint?"
- You: "Yes, let's do it"
- *Work continues immediately*

### **Key Characteristics**

**A Genesis Master Prompt is**:
- ‚úÖ **Comprehensive**: Contains all project context
- ‚úÖ **Living**: Updated after every session
- ‚úÖ **Structured**: Organized for easy reading
- ‚úÖ **Portable**: Works with any LLM

**A Genesis Master Prompt is NOT**:
- ‚ùå **Static**: It evolves with your project
- ‚ùå **Code**: It's documentation, not implementation
- ‚ùå **Tool-specific**: Not locked to one AI platform

## Common Mistakes and Solutions

### **Mistake 1: Not Updating After Every Session**

**Problem**: You forget to update Genesis Master Prompt after a session.

**Result**: Next session loses context, AI doesn't know what was discussed.

**Solution**: Make updating Genesis Master Prompt part of your workflow (like saving a file).

**Tip**: Set a reminder: "Before closing chat, update Genesis Master Prompt!"

---

### **Mistake 2: Too Much Detail**

**Problem**: Genesis Master Prompt becomes 100+ pages, too long to paste.

**Result**: AI can't process it, or you spend too much time maintaining it.

**Solution**: Keep Genesis Master Prompt concise (5-20 pages max).

**Tip**: Move detailed technical specs to separate documents, link from Genesis Master Prompt.

---

### **Mistake 3: Not Documenting Decisions**

**Problem**: You make decisions but don't document them in Genesis Master Prompt.

**Result**: AI suggests the same thing again, you waste time re-discussing.

**Solution**: Always document decisions with rationale.

**Format**:
```markdown
### Decision X: [Topic]
- **What**: [What was decided]
- **Why**: [Rationale]
- **Alternative considered**: [What else was considered]
- **Rationale**: [Why this decision was made]
```

---

### **Mistake 4: Not Using Multiple LLMs**

**Problem**: You only use one LLM (e.g., only Claude).

**Result**: You get single-model bias, miss alternative perspectives.

**Solution**: Use at least 2-3 LLMs for important decisions.

**Recommended**:
- **Kimi**: Innovation and research
- **Claude**: Ethics and safety
- **GPT**: Implementation and coding
- **Gemini**: Analysis and synthesis

---

### **Mistake 5: Treating Genesis Master Prompt as Static**

**Problem**: You create Genesis Master Prompt once and never update it.

**Result**: It becomes outdated, no longer reflects current project state.

**Solution**: Genesis Master Prompt is a **living document** - update it continuously.

**Tip**: Think of it as "project memory" that grows with your project.
